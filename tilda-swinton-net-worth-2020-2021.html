Early Life & Biography

Tilda Swinton was born on 5 November 1960, in the city of London, United Kingdom, to her parents Judith Balfour and Sir John Swinton. She was born in a wealthy family, where her father was a retired major general of the British Army and also served as the Lieutenant of Berwickshire until 2000. Her mother belonged to Australia.

Tilda completed her schooling from Queen’s Gate School and West Heath Girls’ School. She was the classmate of the Princess of Wales, Lady Diana, while studying in West Heath. Tilda later got admission to the University of Cambridge. She completed her graduation with a degree in Social and Political Science. She was a member of the Communist Party while she was in college, and joined the Scottish Socialist Party.

Career

Tilda Swinton started her professional career after she joined the Royal Shakespeare Company, situated in Warwickshire. Her debut play was named as Measure for Measure. Later she joined the Traverse Theatre located in Edinburgh and worked along with Manfred Karge in 1987.

Her first television work was in a 1986 series called Zastrozzi, which was a romantic tale based upon the novel of Percy Bysshe Shelley. Her debut film was titled Caravaggio, which released in the same year. Tilda also won the Best Actress award at the Venice Film Festival in 1991 for her work in the film Edward II.

After gaining fame and popularity, she joined the cast of the Sally Potter films, portraying the role of Orlando. She worked with celebrities such as Leonardo DiCaprio in the movie ‘The Beach(2000)’. She received numerous awards for her contribution to the movie industry.

Tilda was also involved in fashion and collaborated with designers such as Viktor and Rolf. She worked in their show named One Woman Show in 2003. She was also named as one of the fifty best-dressed over 50. Tilda also features in the list of one of the best-dressed women of 2018.
