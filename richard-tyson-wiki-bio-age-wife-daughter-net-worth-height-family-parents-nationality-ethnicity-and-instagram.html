Richard Tyson Biography – Richard Tyson Wiki

Richard Tyson born Richard Martin Tyson is an American actor. He attended Cornell University and graduated with an MFA in Fine Arts.

Richard Tyson Age

Tyson was born on February 13, 1961, in Mobile, Alabama, United States as Richard Martin Tyson.

Richard Tyson Wife

He was married to his wife Tracy Kristofferson. Tracy is the daughter of the legendary actor Kris Kristofferson and his first wife, Frances Mavia Beer. Richard and Tracy have a child together and their marriage ended in divorce in 2017.

Richard Tyson Children – Richard Tyson Daughter

He has a daughter from his marriage to Tracy Kristofferson.

Richard Tyson Parents

Richard’s parents were Senator John Malcolm Tyson and Grace Harris Durant Tyson. His mother died in 2011 at the age of 85. His father died in 2016 at the age of 91.

Richard Tyson Family

Richard has a brother named John Tyson Jr.. John is the former District Attorney of Mobile County. He served for 30 years along with a 14-year stint on the Alabama Board of Education. He was the Democratic Party nominee for Attorney General of Alabama in 2006.

Richard Tyson Height

He stands at a height of 6 feet (1.83 m) tall.

Richard Tyson Net Worth

Tyson has an estimated net worth of $3 million.

Richard Tyson Movies

Richard Tyson’s Wikipedia profile, his most prominent role was as the villain Cullen Crisp Sr. in Kindergarten Cop co-starring alongside Arnold Schwarzenegger. He also starred as high school bully Buddy Revell in the 1987 comedy Three O’Clock High. Tyson can also be seen in the Farrelly Brothers films Kingpin, Me, Myself & Irene and There’s Something About Mary.

Richard was the co-star of the television series Hardball in the 1980s. His roles in the 2000s include The Fear Chamber, Richard III, Flight of the Living Dead, No Bad Days, and the western Shoot First and Pray You Live. He is also the lead in the horror film Big Bad Wolf.

Richard’s acting credits also include guest appearances on the TV shows CSI: NY, Boomtown, Martial Law as well as the films The Visitation, Moscow Heat, Liar’s Poker and Black Hawk Down.
