Biography

Kinky Friedman was born in Chicago, IL on November 1, 1944. Country singer-songwriter, and novelist who has released up to thirteen albums and ran for Governor of Texas in the 2006 election. His song “Sold American” charted at #69. He joined Bob Dylan in 1976 on the second leg of Dylan’s Rolling Thunder Revue Tour. He was an avid chess player when he was younger and, at age seven, he was chosen as the youngest competitor to be one of fifty players to participate in the challenge of U.S. champion, Samuel Reshevsky, in Houston. He went on to earn a Bachelor of Arts in psychology from the University of Texas, Austin in 1966.
