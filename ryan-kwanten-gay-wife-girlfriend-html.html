Ryan Kwanten Gay? Wife-Like Game Changer Girlfriend Is The Answer

Australian actor Ryan Kwanten once showed no signs of dating anyone ever again, let alone having a beautiful wife and a home. Add gay rumors in the mix, Hollywood almost lost one of its most prized assets to bachelorhood.

The 41-year-old from Australia is best known for playing as Jason Stackhouse in True Blood. With his dashing looks and strong features, he has assembled an enormous following online. As of July 2018, over 110k people follow him on Instagram.

He’s currently a lead character in crime drama web series titled The Oath.

Ryan Kwanten's Career; Successfully Transitions Into Hollywood Star 

Ryan began his acting career with stints at various TV Shows: A Country Practice, Hey Dad..! and Spellbinder before he was cast as a regular on Australian soap opera Home and Away in 1997. He left the show in 2002 and moved to America in hopes of making it big in Hollywood.

In 2004, he landed a major role as the character Jay Robertson in Summerland. More roles would soon follow as he appeared in films like Flicka(2006) and Dead Silence(2007).

The University of Sydney alumni 2008 run is believed to be the biggest one of his career as he won the role to play a US Marine accused of raping and murdering in an episode of Law & Order: Special Victims Units and was cast to play Jason Stackhouse in the hit teen vampire series, True Blood in the same hearing.

His Wikipedia reveals he has acted in over 25 films and landed over 18 roles on TV.

Going Back To TV Shows After Hollywood Bound

The actor, who moved to Hollywood 16 years ago after an impressive TV career, looks forward to making it big again to the TV scenarios as he's all set to feature in a new world in Seth Larney’s ‘2067’ in 2019.

Although Kwanten is starring in and serving as one of the producers of The Oath, a crime drama and portraying the role of Steve Hammond, leader of the Ravens gang, he wants to try something new and Seth Larney's '2067' is the perfect suit for him. 

Previously, he also had the pleasure of sharing experiences with Nicolas Cage in Kill Chain, a thriller from Millennium Media and CineTel Films, a tale of betrayal and revenge involving crooked thieves, gangsters, cops, and an ex-mercenary.

Well, all for now, maybe Kwanten is now all focused to making it big in his TV career as he is looking forward to collaborating again with Sen, Seth Larney's '2067' director, next year in Loveland. In the TV series, he will co-star with Hugo Weaving.

So for all the old school Ryan fans, mark your calendars, he is coming soon with a bang in a completely different style in Seth Larney's '2067'. 

Know More About Ryan!

Born on 28 November 1976, Ryan’s middle name is Christian. He was born to a Dutch father, Eddie, and a mother, Kris in Sydney, Australia. He has two brothers: Mitchell and Lloyd who are a musician and a doctor respectively.

Before earning a degree in commerce` from the University of Sydney, the actor/comedian attended St. Paul’s Catholic College in New South Wales.

Famously Infamous Gay Scene In True Blood

The 41-year-old Aussie ex-pat portrays the gay character, Jason Stackhouse in True Blood. Recognizing the show’s substantial gay following, he and his co-stars begged for a gay sex scene between the characters, Jason and Alexander. The now infamous sex scene was aired on the second episode of its penultimate season.

Ryan’s brother, Lloyd is an openly gay doctor. Speaking to reporters about his brother in 2016, he said:

Lloyd was probably about 18 when he came out, and I can wholeheartedly tell you that from the day that he did, he was a changed man for the better.

The True Blood star, however, has never identified himself as a gay man. In fact, he’s been dating actress Ashley Sisino since 2013.
