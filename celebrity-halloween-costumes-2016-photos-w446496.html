Celebrity Halloween Costumes 2016: Photos

Spookiest season! No one loves Halloween more than celebrities — and they didn’t disappoint this year, with some of them going all out on multiple looks.

Not content with a store-bought costume, Taylor Swift borrowed her friend Ryan Reynolds‘ actual Deadpool outfit for a hang with several girlfriends. “You’re the best Deadpool inside contact ever,” the “Love Story” songstress wrote via Instagram, thanking the Proposal actor for the very special favor.

The Pennsylvania native and the Green Lantern star became friends through Blake Lively, who wed Reynolds in 2012. The pop star and the Gossip Girl alum formed a bond in 2015 after Lively publicly confirmed that she’s a Swiftie. Months later, the pair met up in Australia, where they hung out with a koala bear — and the rest is history.

Rather than borrow a look from someone else, Halloween queen Heidi Klum wore something original — then put it on five “doppelgänger” models who accompanied her to her famous party. The supermodel took it further than matching outfits, of course, ensuring that her clones wore prosthetic makeup designed to look like her face.

“Are you seeing double, triple, quadruple, quintuple, sextuple?!” she wrote via Instagram ahead of the big reveal.

The complicated look marked the first time Klum participated in a group costume for her Halloween party. Her past looks have included Jessica Rabbit, Lady Godiva, Betty Boop and Cleopatra.

Other celebrities enlisted their kids for help in completing their costumes. Kelly Rowland and son Titan dressed as hip-hop icons Run-D.M.C., wearing matching hats and Adidas tracksuits. “He’s really starting to understand the holiday,” the Destiny’s Child singer told Yahoo! ahead of the holiday, adding that his 2015 Mickey Mouse costume had really impressed him. “He saw the hands come on, and he thought it was just the most amazing thing he’d ever seen. He saw himself in the mirror and he lost his mind!”

Justin Timberlake and Jessica Biel, meanwhile, accompanied son Silas as characters from the movie Trolls. The “Mirrors” crooner contributed the song “Can’t Stop the Feeling” to the soundtrack and voices the character Branch in the film.
